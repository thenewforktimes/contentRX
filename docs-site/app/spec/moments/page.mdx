# Moments

The 13 moments describe the reader's situation at the point of contact
with a piece of UI copy. They are the second layer of the model, between
content type (what the surface is) and standards (which rules apply).

This page is the narrative intro. For the browsable, linkable taxonomy —
with each moment's weighted standards, example pairs, and per-standard
permalinks — see [the content model](/model).

## The 13 moments

1. **first_encounter** — onboarding, setup, first-run. Clarity above all.
2. **browsing_discovery** — homepages, landing pages, feature tours.
   Scannability matters.
3. **decision_point** — pricing, plan selection, upgrade prompts. No
   dark patterns.
4. **task_execution** — form filling, configuration, multi-step flows.
   Actionable labels.
5. **confirmation** — success, saved, completed. Brevity, passive voice
   is fine.
6. **celebration** — achievement, milestone, streak. Enthusiasm is
   earned, not excessive.
7. **error_recovery** — validation, system errors, failed states. No
   blame, clear next step.
8. **destructive_action** — delete, cancel, irreversible. Explicit
   consequences, friction OK.
9. **empty_state** — zero data, no results. Helpful, suggest next action.
10. **interruption** — modals, toasts, notifications. Brevity critical,
    clear dismiss.
11. **trust_permission** — consent, verification, permissions. Precision
    over warmth, hedging OK.
12. **wayfinding** — navigation, breadcrumbs, section labels.
    Consistency, space-constrained OK.
13. **compliance_disclosure** — regulatory disclaimers, legal mandates,
    FDIC notices. Mandated language takes precedence.

## Why moment-aware

The same string in two different moments often deserves different
verdicts. "Save" on a button in a routine settings flow reads cleanly;
the same word in an emergency shutdown flow reads cold and could miss
the urgency of the moment. Moment-aware evaluation catches that.

See the [whitepaper](/whitepaper) for the longer argument, or [the
content model](/model) to browse the full taxonomy with weights,
examples, and permalinks.
