# What I disagreed with past me about this quarter

_Draft. Anchors to the calibration log entry at `reports/calibration/2026-19.md`._

The first published self-drift number for ContentRX is **κ = 0.57**,
with a 95% confidence interval of [0.31, 0.84]. I disagreed with past
me on 9 of 80 cases. The drift_check tool calls this regime
"degraded" because the point estimate sits below 0.80. I'm publishing
that number anyway, because the alternative is hiding it, and I'd
rather fail and be honest than succeed and be a fraud.

This essay is the why behind the number.

## What the panel measures

The Q2 self-drift panel is 80 strings of UI copy from real products
(Apple, Mailchimp, Wells Fargo, Robinhood, a few Dribbble screenshots,
two healthcare apps). I labeled all 80 last quarter, blind. Today I
labeled the same 80 again, blind. The κ statistic measures how often
current me agreed with past me, accounting for chance.

The panel is the **expert ceiling**. ContentRX cannot be more
consistent with me than I am with myself. If I disagree with past me
11% of the time on these strings, asking the engine to agree with me
90% of the time is asking the engine to be more consistent than the
human ground truth. The honest target ceiling is the κ I draw on this
panel, not the design target of 0.90. This is the rule that keeps the
accuracy page honest.

## What changed in my judgment

Six of the nine disagreements were me **loosening my bar**. Past me
flagged things current me thinks are fine:

- Oxford comma in a marketing list ("a mix of emoji, names, initials
  and numbers"). Past me flagged GRM-01. Current me reads the meaning
  cleanly without it.
- "Phone number not valid" as an error message. Past me thought
  "valid" was too jargon-y. Current me reads it as plain enough.
- "Tell us about your team. Tell us your history. Where are you
  from, what's your name etc." Past me flagged "etc." as a Latin
  abbreviation. Current me thinks it reads fine in a casual form.
- "Get Started" as a button. Past me flagged the title case. Current
  me thinks it's a strong CTA convention.
- "37/40 Places" as a UI label. Past me flagged it as unclear.
  Current me reads it as a normal capacity counter.
- "G2 Logo Red RGB 1" as alt text. Past me flagged the title case.
  Current me thinks alt text on a brand-asset image is allowed to be
  title case if the team rule says so.

Three were me **tightening my bar**:

- "Let us guide you live over video and answer all of your
  questions." Past me let it through. Current me reads it as poorly
  constructed and hard to follow. Better: "Get live guidance over
  video and answers to your questions."
- A solo "20" as a UI label in a celebration screen. Past me passed
  it. Current me thinks a single number with no surrounding label is
  too ambiguous.
- A solo "treatments" lowercase label in the same screen. Past me
  passed it. Current me wants sentence case on standalone words.

The pattern: I loosened my bar on grammar and style technicalities
(Oxford comma, Latin abbreviations, title case) and tightened it on
construction quality and label coherence. That's a coherent shift,
not random drift. It will land in the refinement log as adjustments
to CLR-01, CON-02, GRM-01, GRM-05, PRF-10. ACC-05 too, with caveats
about team-rule overrides.

## What I still agree with myself about

Worth saying: 71 of 80 cases were stable. PRF-03 (trailing period on
a heading) had 17 cases and zero disagreements. ACC-01 (vague link
text) had 4 cases and zero disagreements. Three "Learn more" cases
all called fail, by both past me and current me. The areas where
ContentRX has the most reliable signal are the areas where my
judgment is most stable.

## What this means for the engine

The "degraded" regime classification triggers two operational moves:

1. No new autonomous-tier graduations this quarter. Until the κ
   moves above 0.80, I'm not promoting any standard to "no
   per-verdict review."
2. Existing autonomous standards (we have none yet, so this is a
   no-op for now) would drop to batch-approval until the next
   measurement.

The thresholds adjust to ratios of the measured ceiling: autonomous
κ threshold drops to 0.54, batch-approval to 0.48. These will
recalibrate as the panel does.

## What I'd do differently

A few things I noticed while running through 80 cases:

**The panel is too small.** The 95% CI is [0.31, 0.84]. With 80
cases I cannot tell if the real κ is 0.4 (problematic) or 0.8
(maturing). Q3's panel will target n=150–200 so the band tightens.

**The corpus has at least one quality bug.** A screenshot extraction
from Ditto's roadmap caught a string that was visibly truncated by
the screenshot tool ("so you use. As you use your Ditto style guide
in copy generation,..."). Evaluating it tells me about the engine's
handling of unclear strings, not its handling of finished copy. That
case should be re-captured or removed.

**My judgment notes belong in the refinement log.** I added inline
commentary on roughly 20% of the cases as I labeled them. Those
notes are richer signal than the verdict alone, and they are what
makes the next refinement-log review faster. The Q3 panel UI should
make notes a first-class field.

## Why I'm publishing this

The /accuracy page renders measured numbers honestly: never zero,
never coerced to the design target, pending or measured. Today's
measured self-drift κ = 0.57 with 95% CI [0.31, 0.84] is what the
data says. Publishing 0.85 because it would feel better, or hiding
the number until I can get a tighter result, would be the start of
the slope this product is built to refuse.

The named-expert moat depends on whether you can read what changed
and why. This essay is what changed and why. The next one comes when
the Q3 panel scores.
